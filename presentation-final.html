Project Narrative

What does it mean to design for a landscape that is already toxic?

This project begins with a simple observation: the oil refinery is not disappearing. It is transitioning. As fossil fuel infrastructure enters a period of accelerated decommissioning, vast acreages of contaminated industrial land require new frameworks — not just for cleanup, but for inhabitation, research, and public life.

"Remediation is not the erasure of the industrial past. It is a new form of stewardship."

The project proposes three overlapping conditions across a forty-year horizon: the contamination state (2026), the active remediation state (2040), and the emergent civic condition (2066). These are not sequential phases but simultaneous agencies operating on the same site.

Architecture enters not as monument but as instrument — devices that monitor, filter, and expose the slow processes of ecological recovery, while opening the site to a public long excluded from its operation.

Master Plans

Three Site Conditions

Three overlapping master plans — each revealing a different temporal layer of the same ground. The contamination spread is scroll-triggered on the first view.

Primary contamination plume extends from historic tank farm. Secondary zones associated with cracking units and process areas.

Active remediation strategy combines phytoremediation planting, monitored natural attenuation, and purpose-built monitoring devices. Public access introduced along eastern edge.

By 2066 the site operates as a hybrid research landscape and public park. The monitoring devices have become architectural destinations.

Timeline

From Extraction to Stewardship

A forty-year chronicle of industrial decline and ecological recovery.

1952
Refinery established on site. First crude oil processed. Workforce reaches 600.
1978
Expansion of cracking units. Peak production. First documented groundwater contamination reports suppressed.
2003
Partial decommissioning begins. Regulatory pressure mounts. First public contamination disclosure.
2026 — Now
Full decommissioning. Site enters regulatory consideration. This project begins: a new framework for what comes next.
2030
Remediation devices installed. Research laboratory opens. First public tours of the former refinery.
2040
Phytoremediation reaches 60% of target zones. Data from monitoring towers published as open dataset.
2066
Contamination plume stabilized. Site operates as a public landscape and research destination.
